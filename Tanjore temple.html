The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also known as the Big Temple, was built in the 11th century by Raja Raja Chola I. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Historical Facts

  • Built in 1010 CE by Chola emperor Raja Raja Chola I.
  • Dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as Brihadeeswara.
  • One of the largest temples in India, made entirely of granite.
  • The temple tower (Vimana) rises to 66 meters, among the tallest in the world.
  • Part of the UNESCO World Heritage "Great Living Chola Temples."
