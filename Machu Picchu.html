Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located high in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Historical Facts

  • Built around 1450 under the reign of Inca emperor Pachacuti.
  • Located about 2,430 meters (7,970 ft) above sea level.
  • Abandoned during the Spanish conquest and hidden for centuries.
  • Rediscovered in 1911 by American explorer Hiram Bingham.
  • Known for its sophisticated dry-stone construction blending with natural surroundings.
