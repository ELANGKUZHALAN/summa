Christ the Redeemer is an iconic Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ overlooking Rio de Janeiro from the Corcovado peak. It is one of the most recognized monuments in the world and a symbol of Brazilian culture and faith.

Key Facts

  • Located atop the 710-metre Corcovado mountain in Tijuca National Park, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
  • Statue completed and inaugurated on October 12, 1931.
  • Sculptor: Paul Landowski (French); main engineer: Heitor da Silva Costa (Brazilian); face created by Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida.
  • Statue height: about 30 metres (98 ft); pedestal about 8 metres ‚Äî total roughly 38 metres; arm span approx. 28 metres.
  • Constructed of reinforced concrete and soapstone; major restoration projects carried out periodically.
  • Recognized worldwide as a cultural icon and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
