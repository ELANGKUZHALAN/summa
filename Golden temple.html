The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is the most sacred shrine of Sikhism. Surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar (holy tank), it symbolizes equality, peace, and spiritual devotion.

Historical Facts

  • Located in Amritsar, Punjab, founded by Guru Ram Das in the 16th century.
  • The upper floors of the temple are covered with pure gold leaf, hence the name Golden Temple.
  • The temple is surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar, a sacred pool believed to have healing powers.
  • The Guru Granth Sahib (holy scripture of Sikhs) is housed here.
  • Langar (free community kitchen) serves meals to over 50,000 people daily.
