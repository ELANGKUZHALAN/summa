The Mysore Palace, also known as the Amba Vilas Palace, is a stunning example of Indo-Saracenic architecture. Built in the early 20th century, it was the royal residence of the Wadiyar dynasty.

Historical Facts

  • Current structure built between 1897 and 1912 by the Wadiyars.
  • Designed by British architect Henry Irwin in Indo-Saracenic style.
  • Illuminated with nearly 100,000 bulbs during Dussehra celebrations.
  • One of the most visited palaces in India, after the Taj Mahal.
  • Contains beautiful courtyards, gardens, and temples within the complex.
