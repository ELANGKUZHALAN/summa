The Taj Mahal, built in 1648 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, is a symbol of love and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Made of white marble, it attracts millions of visitors every year.

Historical Facts

  • Commissioned in 1632 and completed in 1648.
  • Built as a mausoleum for Mumtaz Mahal, wife of Shah Jahan.
  • Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.
  • Famous for its stunning white marble and symmetrical design.
