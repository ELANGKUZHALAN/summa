The Great Stupa at Sanchi is one of the oldest stone structures in India. Commissioned by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE, it is a major Buddhist pilgrimage site.

Historical Facts

  • Originally built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE.
  • The stupa enshrines relics of the Buddha.
  • Known for its beautifully carved gateways, or Toranas.
  • A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1989.
  • Represents the spread of Buddhism in India and beyond.
