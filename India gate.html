The India Gate, inaugurated in 1931, is a war memorial dedicated to the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives during World War I. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it stands proudly on Rajpath in New Delhi.

Historical Facts

  • Foundation stone laid in 1921, completed in 1931.
  • Designed by British architect Sir Edwin Lutyens.
  • Names of over 13,000 soldiers inscribed on its walls.
  • Amar Jawan Jyoti was added in 1972 after the Indo-Pak war.
  • A symbol of sacrifice and national pride.
