The Meenakshi Amman Temple is a historic Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareswarar. Famous for its towering gopurams and vibrant sculptures, it is a symbol of Tamil culture and devotion.

Historical Facts

  • Located on the banks of the Vaigai River in Madurai, Tamil Nadu.
  • Dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (Parvati) and Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva).
  • Famous for its 14 colorful gopurams with thousands of detailed sculptures.
  • Originally built by the Pandya kings, expanded by Nayak rulers in the 16th century.
  • One of the "New Seven Wonders of the World" nominees.
